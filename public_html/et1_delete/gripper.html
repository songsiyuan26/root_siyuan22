My 3D Printing Project This is my basic introduction to my 3D printing project, a gripper model. I will break down the operational steps, material requirements, and component details. 2. Component List and Material Selection The key components of the gripper include - Gears The gears control the mechanical transmission that opens and closes the gripper. The size and number of teeth on the gears need to be selected based on the gripping force and the opening angle of the gripper. - Gripper claws The gripping part of the tool. - Bearings and pins These connect the gears to the gripper claws and must rotate smoothly. It is important to measure the appropriate diameter and length to ensure tight fitting between the components. Project Features 1. Precise Gear Transmission System The core of the gripper is its gear transmission mechanism. By designing gears of different sizes and tooth counts, I ensure that the gripper can precisely control its opening and closing, providing a stable grip force. 2. Modular Design My gripper project consists of multiple modules, including the gripping claws, gears, bearings, and support frame, which can be easily assembled or replaced. Operation The gripper's operation mainly relies on its gear transmission system. When one gear is rotated or driven by an external force, the other gears are linked, causing the gripper claws to open and close simultaneously.
